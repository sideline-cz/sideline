The carpool board lets your team coordinate who is driving and who needs a lift — all inside Discord, with live updates. A captain posts the board once; members add cars and reserve seats using buttons.

## Posting the board

Only members with the `carpool:manage` permission (Admin and Captain by default) can post a board.

<Steps>

1. Go to the Discord channel where you want the board to appear.
2. Type `/doprava` (or `/carpool` in English) and press Enter.
3. The bot posts a public **Carpool board** embed in the channel with an **Add a car** button. Only you see the ephemeral "board created" confirmation — the embed itself is visible to everyone.

</Steps>

<Aside type="note" title="One board per command">
  Each `/doprava` invocation creates a separate board. If you run the command twice you get two independent boards. There is no automatic cleanup of old boards.
</Aside>

## Adding a car

Any team member (not just captains) can offer a lift by adding a car.

<Steps>

1. Click **Add a car** on the board embed.
2. A small form appears. Enter the **capacity** — this is the total number of people the car fits, **including yourself as the driver**. For example, enter `4` if you can take yourself plus 3 passengers.
3. Click **Submit**. The bot adds your car to the board and opens a **private thread** for your car.

</Steps>

The capacity must be between 1 and 8 (inclusive). You can only have one car per board.

### Your car's private thread

After you add a car the bot creates a private Discord thread — only you and the passengers in your car can see it. The thread contains five buttons:

- **Assign passenger** — pick a team member from a Discord user-select menu to add them to your car on their behalf.
- **Leave** — for passengers to leave the car directly from the thread (see [Leaving a car](#leaving-a-car)).
- **Remove car** — remove your car entirely (see [Removing a car](#removing-a-car)).
- **Change seats** — update how many seats your car has (see [Changing your seat count](#changing-your-seat-count-owner-only)).
- **Kick passenger** — remove a specific passenger from your car (see [Removing a passenger](#removing-a-passenger-owner-only)).

You are automatically added to the thread when the car is created.

<Aside type="note" title="Car numbers">
  Cars are numbered in the order they were added — the first car offered on a board is always **Car 1**, and new cars append as the next number. Removing a car does shift the numbers of any cars added after it.
</Aside>

<Aside type="note" title="Thread creation">
  If Discord cannot create the thread (for example, because of channel permissions), your car is still added to the board — it just won't have a private thread. Everything else continues to work normally.
</Aside>

## Reserving a seat

Any team member can reserve an available seat in a car by clicking its **Reserve** button on the public board.

- You are added to the car's private thread automatically so you can follow the driver's updates.
- The board refreshes to show the updated passenger list.
- You can be in at most one car per board. Clicking **Reserve** while you already have a seat in a different car on the same board will show an error.
- If you own a car on this board, you cannot also reserve a passenger seat in another car on the same board.
- The driver cannot reserve a seat in their own car.

After reserving, an ephemeral confirmation appears with a **Leave** button in case you change your mind immediately.

## Leaving a car

You can leave a car in three ways:

- Click **Leave** in the ephemeral confirmation that appears right after you reserve a seat.
- Click **Leave** in the car's private thread (the persistent button between Assign and Remove).
- Click **Leave my car** on the public carpool board — this resolves your current car automatically, so you do not need to find the right thread.

In all cases:

- You are removed from the car's private thread.
- The board refreshes to show the updated passenger list.

<Aside type="caution">
  Car **owners cannot leave** their own car. To remove yourself from the board, use **Remove car** in the private thread instead.
</Aside>

## Assigning a passenger (owner only)

If someone tells you they need a lift but cannot click the button themselves, you can add them manually from your car's private thread.

<Steps>

1. Open your car's private thread.
2. Click **Assign passenger**.
3. Select the team member from the user-select menu.
4. Click **Submit**. The selected member is added to your car and to the thread.

</Steps>

The assigned member must be a Sideline team member on the same server. They will see the confirmation in the thread. They cannot be the car owner, and they cannot already have a seat in another car on the same board.

## Removing a car

Car owners can remove their car from the board at any time using the **Remove car** button in the private thread.

- All passenger seats in the car are removed.
- The private thread is archived and locked.
- The public board refreshes to show the car is gone.

<Aside type="caution">
  Removing a car is permanent. Passengers are ejected from the thread and will need to reserve a seat in another car.
</Aside>

## Changing your seat count (owner only)

If your plans change — for example, you find room for one more passenger, or someone drops out and you want fewer seats shown as available — you can update your car's capacity at any time.

<Steps>

1. Open your car's private thread.
2. Click **Change seats**.
3. Enter the new total number of seats (including yourself as the driver).
4. Click **Submit**. The board refreshes to show the new capacity.

</Steps>

The new value must be between 1 and 8 (inclusive). You cannot reduce the seat count below the number of people currently in the car (owner plus passengers) — remove a passenger first if you need to go lower.

## Removing a passenger (owner only)

If a passenger asks to drop out but cannot click **Leave** themselves, or you need to free up their seat for someone else, you can remove them directly.

<Steps>

1. Open your car's private thread.
2. Click **Kick passenger**.
3. Select the passenger from the user-select menu.
4. Click **Submit**. The passenger is removed from your car and from the private thread.

</Steps>

The removed passenger is not notified privately, but they will no longer see the thread and the board's passenger list updates immediately. You can only remove someone who currently has a seat in your car.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely cause |
|---------|--------------|
| `/doprava` does not appear | You do not have the `carpool:manage` permission. Ask your captain or admin. |
| "Car is full" error | All seats (including the driver's) are taken. Find another car. |
| "Already in another car" error | You already have a seat in a different car on this board, or you own a car on this board. A member can only be in one car per board (as driver or passenger, not both). |
| Cannot see the private thread | You haven't reserved a seat (or been assigned one) in that car yet. |
| "Seats can't be fewer than the people already in the car" error | You tried to reduce your car's capacity below its current number of occupants. Remove a passenger first, then lower the seat count. |
| "That person isn't in this car" error | The passenger you selected in **Kick passenger** doesn't currently have a seat in your car (they may have already left). |
