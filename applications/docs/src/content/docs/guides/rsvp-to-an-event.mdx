Every event you're invited to gets an RSVP prompt — in Discord, on the web, or both. Responding is a single click.

## What the responses mean

- **Yes** — I'll be there. Counts as present for attendance stats.
- **No** — I won't be there. Counts as an explicit decline, not a no-show.
- **Maybe** — I'm not sure yet. Counts as undecided; you'll get a reminder closer to the event.

The key difference between **No** and silence: **No** tells your captain you got the message. Silence doesn't. That's why reminders keep nudging people who haven't answered.

<Aside type="note" title="Reminders stop after repeated non-responses">
  If you have a **Player** role and miss a configurable number of events in a row without responding, the bot stops sending you RSVP reminder DMs. Respond to any event — yes, no, or maybe — to reset your streak and start receiving reminders again. Your captain can adjust the threshold in **Team settings → RSVP reminders → Stop reminding after N missed RSVPs**.
</Aside>

## Responding in Discord

<Steps>

1. Find the event post in your team's events channel. It shows the date, time, location, and current counts.
2. Click **Yes**, **No**, or **Maybe**. The buttons are under the event details.
3. The post updates immediately with your name in the right bucket.

</Steps>

## Responding on the web

<Steps>

1. Open the Sideline web app and go to your team's Events tab.
2. Click an event to see details.
3. Click the RSVP button you want. The count updates live.

</Steps>

## Changing your mind

You can change your RSVP at any time until the event starts. Click a different button; the previous answer is overwritten. Captains see the change immediately.

<Aside type="caution" title="After the event">
  Once the event has ended, RSVPs are locked for attendance records. If you need to mark yourself as present after the fact (e.g. you forgot to click), ask your captain to adjust it from the event page.
</Aside>

## What if I'm running late?

RSVPing Yes doesn't mean you promise to be on time. If you're running late, say so in the team's Discord. Sideline doesn't track late arrivals — that's on your team's culture.
