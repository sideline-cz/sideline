Sideline lets every team member log personal physical activity — gym sessions, runs, stretches — and tracks progress over time. Consistent effort unlocks **achievements**, some of which grant a special Discord role on your team's server.

## Logging activity

You can log activity in two ways:

- **From Discord:** use the `/makanicko log` slash command. Choose an activity type and optionally provide a duration, a note, and a date.
- **From the web app:** open your member profile → **Activity** tab → **Log activity**.

Each log entry records the activity type, the date, an optional duration in minutes, and an optional free-text note.

### Backdating and future-dating entries

By default an entry is stamped with today's date. You can pick a different date — up to 2 years in the past or 2 years in the future — using the **Date** picker in the web form or the `date` option in the Discord command. Dates always anchor at noon Europe/Prague time, so they land in the correct streak and stats bucket regardless of when you submit the form.

<Aside type="note" title="Auto-logged entries">
  Entries created automatically (for example, when a training event ends and you had RSVP'd yes — or coming later, which also counts as attending) always use the training's own date. These entries cannot be backdated or edited.
</Aside>

<Aside type="note" title="Available activity types">
  The four global built-in types are **Gym**, **Running**, **Stretching**, and **Training**. Your team admins and captains may define additional custom types. All types (built-in and custom) appear in the web app's activity log form and as autocomplete choices in the `/makanicko log` command.
</Aside>

## Managing activity types (admins and captains)

Team admins and captains can define custom activity types in addition to the four global built-ins.

Go to **Team → Activity types** in the web app to:

- **View** all activity types available to your team, including built-ins and any custom ones you have created.
- **Create** a new type by entering a name (unique within your team, max 50 characters) and, optionally, an emoji and a short description.
- **Edit** the name, emoji, or description of a custom type. Global built-in types cannot be edited.
- **Delete** a custom type that has not been used in any activity log yet. Built-in types and types with existing log entries cannot be deleted.

<Aside type="note" title="Usage count">
  Each type shows a usage count — the number of log entries recorded against it. Use this to decide whether it is safe to delete a type you no longer need.
</Aside>

## Viewing stats

Run `/makanicko stats` in Discord or open your member profile in the web app to see:

- **Current streak** — consecutive days with at least one logged activity.
- **Longest streak** — the best streak you have ever achieved.
- **Total activities** and **total duration**.
- A per-type breakdown of activity counts.
- Your earned achievements.

## Achievements

Achievements are milestone badges earned automatically when your activity stats hit a threshold. Each achievement is awarded once and cannot be lost.

| Achievement | Unlocked when |
|---|---|
| First activity | You log your first activity |
| Ten activities | You reach 10 logged activities |
| Fifty activities ⭐ | You reach 50 logged activities |
| Hundred activities ⭐ | You reach 100 logged activities |
| 3-day streak | Your longest streak reaches 3 days |
| 7-day streak ⭐ | Your longest streak reaches 7 days |
| 30-day streak ⭐ | Your longest streak reaches 30 days |
| 600 minutes | Your total logged duration reaches 600 minutes |
| 3000 minutes ⭐ | Your total logged duration reaches 3000 minutes |
| Gym 25 | You log 25 gym sessions |
| Running 25 | You log 25 running sessions |

Achievements marked ⭐ **can grant a Discord role** if your team admin has configured a role mapping for them.

## Discord role rewards

When you earn an achievement marked ⭐, the bot automatically checks whether a Discord role has been configured for that achievement in your team. If one has, the bot:

1. Grants the Discord role to your account on the team's server.
2. Posts a congratulatory message in the team's welcome channel (if one is set) tagging you and the role.

If no role mapping or no welcome channel is configured, the achievement is still recorded — you just won't see a Discord announcement.

<Aside type="note" title="Admins: managing achievements">
  Go to **Team → Achievements** in the web app to manage achievements. From there you can:

  - **Adjust thresholds** for built-in achievements — use the preview to see how many members would qualify before saving.
  - **Create custom achievements** with your own name, description, rule (total activities, longest streak, total duration, or per-activity-type count), and threshold.
  - **Link a Discord role** to any achievement. Choose an existing role by ID, or select **Auto-create** to have the bot find or create a role with the achievement's name automatically.
</Aside>

## Weekly summary

Every Sunday at 20:00 in your team's configured timezone, the bot posts a **weekly activity summary** embed to a Discord channel configured by your captain.

The embed includes:

- Total activities and total logged duration for the week.
- Number of active members (those who logged at least one activity).
- A leaderboard of top contributors with their activity count and duration.
- New achievements earned by any team member during the week.
- A comparison with the previous week's activity total.

You can also view your personal weekly summary any time in the web app at **Workout → Weekly summary**. The page shows your own activity breakdown by type, current and longest streaks, new achievements, and a comparison with the previous week. If you are a captain, the page also shows the full team breakdown.

<Aside type="note" title="Captains: enabling the weekly summary">
  Go to **Team settings → Discord integration** and set the **Weekly summary channel** field to the Discord channel where the embed should be posted. When no channel is set the summary is not sent.
</Aside>
