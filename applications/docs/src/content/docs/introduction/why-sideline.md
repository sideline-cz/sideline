title: Why Sideline?
description: The problems Sideline solves and how it solves them.

Most amateur teams run on a messy mix of Discord threads, WhatsApp groups, Google Sheets, and whoever happens to keep the roster in their head. Sideline replaces that with one system that lives inside Discord.

## Problems we solve

### RSVP chaos

Three threads, two polls, and a "who's in tonight?" message at 8 PM. Sideline puts a single RSVP next to each event with three buttons: Yes, No, Coming later. Captains see the count live.

### Roster drift

Who's actually on the team this month? Sideline has one canonical roster with roles (player, captain, admin) and groups (first team, reserves, under-17s). It keeps Discord roles in sync so channel access matches reality.

### Event spam

Recurring events shouldn't mean 20 duplicate posts a month. Sideline creates a single event series, posts each occurrence at the right time, and can delete or archive old ones when they're done.

### Forgotten reminders

The last people to answer are always the same. Sideline sends reminders automatically — before the RSVP deadline and after, pinging only the people who still haven't replied.

## What makes Sideline different

- **Discord-first, not Discord-optional.** We don't treat Discord as an afterthought — it's the primary UI. The web app is for setup and admin, not daily use.
- **Built for amateur clubs.** Not students, not pro teams, not LAN parties. The vocabulary and defaults assume training nights, weekend games, and mixed-age rosters.
- **Free and open source.** Host it yourself if you want, or use our hosted instance. Either way, your data is yours.
